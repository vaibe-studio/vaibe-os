# Современный русский текст - Ильяхов и публицистика

Reference knowledge for writing strong modern Russian prose that combines editorial clarity with literary-publicistic texture.

## Purpose

This note captures a practical synthesis:

- **Ильяхов** useful as a system of clarity, structure, trust and editorial discipline
- **Публицистика** useful as a source of rhythm, imagery, narrative pressure and voice

The goal is **not** to flatten prose into sterile infostyle. The goal is to keep the text alive, intelligent and memorable while making every sentence work.

## Core principle

Strong modern Russian prose is built on two layers:

1. **Load-bearing layer**: meaning, structure, fact, reader orientation
2. **Expressive layer**: rhythm, metaphor, irony, contrast, voice

If expressive techniques appear without the load-bearing layer, the text becomes foggy.
If the load-bearing layer exists without expressive technique, the text becomes dry.

The desired result is: **ясный, сильный, образный текст без рыхлости и без фальши**.

## What to take from Ilyakhov

Based on `Информационный стиль`, lectures on strong text, title, structure, useful action, facts vs evaluations, and editing from top down:

- **Useful action first**: the text must do something for the reader, not merely sound good
- **Meaning before decoration**: every paragraph has a job
- **Facts instead of imposed conclusions**: show the material so the reader reaches the conclusion with the author
- **Top-down editing**: first purpose and structure, then paragraphs, then sentences, then words
- **One semantic movement per paragraph**: paragraph is a unit of thought, not a visual chunk
- **Readability by syntax**: complex thought is allowed, but syntax must remain traversable
- **Reduce false significance**: avoid pomp, clichés, vague intensifiers and empty grandeur
- **Trust through concreteness**: dates, actions, scenes, examples, mechanisms, consequences
- **Title as upper-level structure**: title should open a real question or promise a real resolution
- **Hook through tension**: paradox, scene, question, conflict, missing explanation

## What NOT to misunderstand in Ilyakhov

Ilyakhov is often reduced to crude simplifications. Avoid these distortions:

- Not "all sentences must be short"
- Not "all metaphors are bad"
- Not "literary tone is forbidden"
- Not "remove personality from prose"
- Not "replace style with office clarity"

Correct reading:

- remove the **weak** metaphor, not all metaphor
- remove the **false** emotion, not emotional power
- remove the **empty** adjective, not precision of tone
- remove the **imposed** conclusion, not the author's position

## Synthesis with literary-publicistic writing

### 1. Build the skeleton first

Before polishing language, define:

- What is the central thesis?
- Why should the reader care?
- What tension keeps the text alive?
- What changes in the reader after the text?

If this is not clear, language polish will only hide structural weakness.

### 2. Open with a live intellectual tension

The first lines should create a quiet but real internal question.

Good entry points:

- **Paradox**: "человек с манерами хулигана дисциплинировал вкус эпохи"
- **Scene**: small concrete episode that unfolds a larger theme
- **Personal stake**: why the subject matters to the author now
- **Gap in explanation**: "все слова о нём верны, но ни одно не объясняет главного"

Weak openings:

- abstract declarations
- generic praise
- "сегодня я хочу рассказать"
- throat-clearing introductions

### 3. Let each paragraph push

Each paragraph should do one of four things:

- introduce a tension
- reveal a mechanism
- give a vivid fact or image
- sharpen the conclusion

If a paragraph merely sounds good, cut or rebuild it.

### 4. Use images as instruments, not ornaments

Prefer images that clarify mechanism:

- factory
- bridge
- forge
- door
- school
- machine

Good image:

- compresses complexity
- can hold several later paragraphs
- helps the reader think, not just admire

### 5. Prefer scene and mechanism over praise

Instead of:

- "он великий"
- "он гениальный"
- "он очень глубокий"

Prefer:

- what he built
- how he acted
- what changed in the environment
- what contradiction he managed to hold together

### 6. Keep authorial position, but do not force verdicts

The author may have admiration, gratitude, irritation, tenderness, irony.
But the prose should not beg the reader to agree.

Better:

- stage the evidence
- choose exact verbs
- give the reader room to arrive

### 7. Long sentences are allowed; confusion is not

Russian publicistic prose can breathe through long sentences.
But long syntax should preserve:

- visible grammatical spine
- chronological order where possible
- limited nesting
- one main semantic arc

Rule of thumb:

- long sentence for expansion, contrast, culmination
- short sentence for удар, reset, conclusion

### 8. Rhythm is part of meaning

Alternate:

- dense analytical paragraphs
- lighter bridging sentences
- short ударные conclusions

Useful rhythm moves:

- triad
- antithesis
- parcellation
- delayed predicate
- rhetorical return to the main question

Avoid rhythmic monotony: same sentence length, same paragraph shape, same emotional temperature.

### 9. Humor and kindness sharpen better than self-defense

When writing about a large and controversial figure:

- do not become their lawyer
- do not sterilize sharp edges
- do not moralize

Instead:

- name the edge
- turn it slightly through wit
- keep benevolence
- preserve scale

Formula:

**острота без злобы, уважение без сервильности**

### 10. End not with repetition, but with elevation

A good ending should:

- resolve the opening tension
- widen the scale
- leave a quotable line

Weak ending:

- summary of things already said

Strong ending:

- final redefinition
- gratitude with earned authority
- wish, verdict, or image that feels inevitable

## Hook patterns for essays and profiles

### Pattern A: paradox

Open with two qualities that should not coexist, then ask how they do.

### Pattern B: generational recognition

Open with what the figure changed in the internal norm of a generation.

### Pattern C: incomplete explanation

List all obvious labels, then show they fail to explain the essence.

### Pattern D: personal stake

Open with why the author cannot approach the subject coldly.

## Packaging longform for Telegram

When a literary-publicistic text becomes too large for a Telegram post, do not compress the whole article into a bloated caption.

Preferred pattern:

1. **Telegram post as doorway**
2. **Telegraph as full essay**
3. **Image as tonal frame**

### What belongs in the Telegram post

- one strong opening paragraph
- one or two short paragraphs with the core paradox or tension
- one clear promise of what the full text resolves
- `Читать здесь`
- link
- a small set of tags

### What should NOT happen

- retell the whole article in miniature
- over-explain every nuance
- overload the post with structure markers
- force the reader through a wall of text inside Telegram

### Working formula

`Hook`
`Context`
`Central paradox or question`
`One strong thought`
`Read here + link`
`Tags`

### Editorial principle

Telegram post is not a reduced article.
It is a **threshold** into the article.

The post should already have independent value, but leave an unresolved charge that naturally sends the reader to the longform.

## Visual workflow for essay illustrations

For reflective essays and cultural profiles, literal portrait posters are often weaker than indirect editorial imagery.

Prefer iterative visual development:

1. **Define the channel mood first**
2. **Choose a symbolic scene, not just a face**
3. **Move from literal to indirect framing**
4. **Refine through 2-3 controlled iterations**

### Strong choices for editorial illustration

- profile or semi-back view
- window, reflection, threshold, road, map, room, screen, corridor
- environment that reveals the subject indirectly
- mood as primary carrier of meaning

### Weak choices

- frontal heroic portrait without need
- corporate poster energy
- over-literal iconography
- cluttered interface screens used as decoration only

### Guiding rule

The image should not merely depict the person.
It should depict the **way the person stands in relation to a world**.

## Checklist before finalizing a text

- Is there a real central thesis?
- Does the first paragraph open a question?
- Does every paragraph perform a distinct task?
- Are there concrete facts or mechanisms behind major claims?
- Are the strongest images structural, not decorative?
- Is the admiration or critique shown through material, not pressure?
- Does the syntax breathe rather than tangle?
- Does the ending elevate rather than merely stop?

## Heuristic for editing

Edit in this order:

1. **Purpose** — what the text does
2. **Structure** — how the argument or narrative moves
3. **Paragraphs** — one paragraph, one push
4. **Sentences** — clarity, rhythm, contrast
5. **Words** — precision, tone, cliché removal

Never start by polishing adjectives in a weak structure.
